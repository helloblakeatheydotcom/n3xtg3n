Decorative Color

Defines the decorative palettes used for rails, badge and tag variation, and accent treatments that add visual range without adding shared system meaning.

Rails Badge and tag variants Accent families

Overview

Decorative colors provide visual variation but do not communicate system state on their own.

Use them to theme rails, vary badges and tags, and introduce accent families where the goal is distinction, tone, or visual interest rather than shared semantic meaning.

Guidance

Use decorative color when variation, theme, or distinction is the goal. Use semantic color when the interface must communicate shared meaning such as state, feedback, interaction, or accessibility treatment.

Use Decorative Color For

  • Theming rails and accentsSection rails, decorative stripes, and accent families that help neighboring content feel distinct.
  • Adding categorical variationBadge and tag variants that broaden the palette without introducing shared semantic meaning.
  • Adding flair without stateSurfaces or accents that should feel expressive but should not imply success, warning, danger, or interaction.

Use Semantic Color For

  • Interaction and focusPrimary actions, hover states, active states, focus rings, and interactive surfaces.
  • Feedback and validationInfo, success, warning, and danger treatments for notices, messages, errors, and destructive states.
  • Shared system meaningAny color that needs to carry a reusable meaning across components belongs in the semantic layer, not the decorative layer.

Accent Families

These decorative families can be used for rails, supporting accents, and non-semantic visual variation. They should not replace semantic tokens for status, feedback, interaction, or accessibility states.

Neutral and Supporting

Slate, Sand, and Coffee

Use for quieter decorative rails, earthy themed categories, or softer visual grouping.

Cool Accent

Violet, Sky, and Indigo

Use when a themed surface needs more visual range than the semantic blue interaction system provides.

Fresh Accent

Emerald and Teal

Use for distinct but non-semantic visual categories that should feel fresh, calm, or environmental.

Warm Accent

Amber, Cayenne, and Rose

Use sparingly for high-energy decorative accents that should stand out without implying feedback state.

Decorative Rails

Rail colors are used for decorative section accents. They provide variation and help adjacent content feel distinct, but they do not communicate system state on their own.
