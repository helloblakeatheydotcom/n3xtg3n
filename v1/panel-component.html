Panel

Panels are designed for structured section-level content. They provide a strong container for grouping related information, controls, and actions with clear hierarchy.

Standard Panel

The standard panel is the most common layout pattern. It includes a header, body, and optional footer. Panels may be collapsed when sections of content are optional or frequently hidden. Collapsed/Expanded state should persist when possible.

Panel Overview

Panels group related content into clear, bounded sections. A panel may include an optional header, a required body, an optional footer, and optional header actions. Panels can be static when content should always remain visible, or collapsible when sections benefit from being hidden and revealed on demand.

Variants and Optional Elements

Supported Patterns

  • Standard panel
  • Panel without footer
  • Static panel
  • Panel with form content
  • Panel with header actions

Optional Elements

  • Header link button
  • Header menu toggle
  • Collapse toggle
  • Footer actions

Specifications

Structure

  • Body is required.
  • Header and footer are optional.
  • For collapsible panels, the collapse toggle sits at the far left of the header.
  • Header actions align to the right edge of the header.

Sizing

  • Header minimum height is 64px.
  • Footer minimum height is 56px.
  • Collapse controls use a 32px square hit area.

Behavior

  • The header acts as the collapse trigger except where action buttons and the collapse control are present.
  • Collapsible panels rotate the chevron 90 degrees between expanded and collapsed states.
  • Collapsed panels hide the body and footer content.
  • Interactive header controls do not trigger collapse.

Accessibility

  • Use aria-expanded and aria-controls on the collapse toggle.
  • Menus close on outside click and when Escape is pressed.
  • Header actions remain individually interactive inside clickable headers.
