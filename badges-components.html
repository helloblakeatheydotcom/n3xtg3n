Badges and Tags

Badges and tags are compact supporting labels that sit alongside record content. Badge color pairs and tag color pairs must follow the approved combinations on the Colors page so category and status treatments stay consistent across the system.

Badges vs Tags

Use badges for category or type. Use tags for status or state. They can appear together on a record, but they do not replace one another.

Do not stack multiple badges or multiple tags on a single record. A record may show one badge, one tag, one of each, or neither.

Overview

Badges and tags are compact metadata treatments that sit in the top row of components like cards and record rows. They are not interchangeable: badges communicate category, and tags communicate status.

Anatomy

A badge is a rounded category pill. A tag is a square-corner status label with a colored dot. When both appear together, separate them with a thin divider.

Specifications

These rules define how badges and tags should be used across the system.

Usage Rules

  • Badges are for categories, types, or delivery models.
  • Tags are for status or state.
  • A record may show one badge, one tag, one of each, or neither.
  • Do not place multiple badges on a single record.
  • Do not place multiple tags on a single record.
  • When both appear together, separate them with a thin divider.
