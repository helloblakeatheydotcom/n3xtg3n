Panels

Structured section containers for content, controls, actions, and form workflows. Defines panel structure, content rhythm, header actions, and collapsible behavior for grouped section content.

Overview

Panels group related content into clear, bounded sections. A panel includes a required header, a required body, an optional footer, and optional header actions. Panels can be static when content should always remain visible, or collapsible when sections benefit from being hidden and revealed on demand.

Core Structure

Body is requiredThe panel body is the only mandatory region and holds the section's primary content.

Header is requiredEvery panel includes a header so the section always has a title and a consistent top structure for optional icons, collapse controls, or actions.

Footer is optionalUse the footer only when actions or summary controls belong to the panel itself.

Layout owns widthThe surrounding layout controls overall width while the panel remains width: 100% with a max-width tokenized elsewhere.

Panel Behavior

Static or collapsibleUse static panels for content that should always remain visible and collapsible panels for optional or secondary sections.

Actions stay localHeader and footer actions should belong to the panel content rather than the page shell.

Consistent rhythmHeader, body, and footer spacing should stay predictable across content-only and form-heavy panels.

State should persistCollapsed and expanded state should persist when possible so the interface respects the user’s previous choices.

Guidance

Panels are the primary container for process-page content. Use them to group related data together into meaningful sections, not as wrappers for isolated controls.

How To Group Content

Group like fields and related controls together so each panel represents a meaningful section of the workflow.

Use a panel when the content benefits from a shared title, optional actions, or a clear boundary from adjacent process content.

Structure process pages as a series of purposeful panels rather than leaving fields loose in the page layout.

Aim for panels that feel substantial enough to scan as sections, not incidental wrappers around a single control.

Panel Rules

Do not use a panel merely to hold a single field, a single button, or one lightweight control.

Do not simulate a footer with empty space when no actions are needed.

Keep header actions lightweight and scoped to the panel.

Panels often contain grouped form fields. Keep the internal layout simple and avoid creating another container system inside the panel.

Maintain consistent spacing between stacked panels and between content rows inside the body.

Anatomy

A panel combines a required header with a required content region. Headers may include a collapse control and actions, while footers are reserved for actions that belong to the panel itself.

The header and body are required. The footer is optional. Actions live on the right side of the header, and footer actions align to the right edge of the panel.

Parts

Container

Primary section wrapper with a subtle border, panel radius, and low-elevation shadow.

Header

Required top region for the title and the consistent placement of optional icons, collapse controls, and header actions.

Title

Section label that anchors the content and helps the page scan as a sequence of grouped sections.

Body

Required content region that holds the panel’s main text, fields, or grouped controls.

Footer

Optional bottom region for panel-level actions when the section needs an explicit outcome.

Interaction

Panels may be static or collapsible, but the behavior should stay predictable across examples and production usage.

Collapsible Behavior

The collapse and expand control lives in the header and uses aria-expanded and aria-controls.

The header may act as the click target when embedded actions preserve their own click behavior.

The chevron rotates to indicate collapsed and expanded state.

Collapsed panels hide the body and footer content together.

Action Behavior

Header link buttons and menus stay interactive without triggering collapse.

Menus close on outside click and when Escape is pressed.

Footer actions align to the right and should use the primary button treatment when the action is the main panel outcome.

Hover behavior should remain subtle: the panel border darkens slightly without introducing a heavier shadow treatment.
